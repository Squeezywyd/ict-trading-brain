# Entry Rules

## Definition
Entry Rules are the **non-negotiable conditions that must be met before any position is opened**. These rules exist to eliminate emotional entries and ensure every trade has the structural, timing, and confluence requirements of a valid ICT setup.

An entry is NOT valid if even one of the core conditions is missing. The discipline to wait for all conditions is what separates consistent traders from random ones.

## Rules

### Step 1 — Confirm HTF Bias
- Read the 4H or 1D chart first — see [[Higher Timeframe Bias]]
- Identify the current structural direction (bullish or bearish)
- Only trade in that direction unless a clear [[Change of Character]] has confirmed a flip

### Step 2 — Identify the Kill Zone
- Check the clock — is it London open (08:00–11:00 GMT) or NY open (13:30–16:00 GMT)?
- If outside a [[Kill Zones|kill zone]], wait — do not enter
- Mark the [[Asian Session Range]] high and low before London open

### Step 3 — Identify the Setup
- Look for a [[Liquidity Sweep]] of a key level (swing high/low, equal highs/lows)
- Wait for a [[Displacement Candle]] following the sweep
- Identify the resulting [[Fair Value Gap]] or [[IFVG]] zone
- Alternatively: locate a clean, untouched [[Order Block (Bullish)|OB]] at a discount/premium level

### Step 4 — Confirm Entry
- Wait for price to return to the FVG, IFVG, or OB zone
- Require a **rejection candle** inside the zone: pin bar, engulfing, or inside bar break
- Check [[SMT Divergence]] on the correlated pair if available
- Ensure the zone is inside [[Discount Zone]] (longs) or [[Premium Zone]] (shorts)

### Step 5 — Execute with Defined Risk
- Set stop loss beyond the entry zone — see [[Stop Loss Placement]]
- Set TP1 at nearest [[Internal Liquidity]] and TP2 at [[External Liquidity]]
- Verify that R:R is minimum 1:2 — if not, skip the trade
- Risk maximum 1% of account — see [[Risk Management]]
