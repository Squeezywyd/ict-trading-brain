## Five Maps of Content

Each MOC is a themed hub linking to 10+ concept notes. Pick the topic you're studying.

---

### [[Liquidity & Sweeps - MOC]]
*Where stops live and how they get hunted.*
Covers: buy-side & sell-side liquidity, equal highs/lows, stop hunts, displacement, the sweep mechanism, and how to identify the next draw on liquidity. The mechanical foundation of every ICT setup.

---

### [[Imbalances & Gaps - MOC]]
*The entry zones: FVG, IFVG, and everything in between.*
Covers: fair value gaps, inverse fair value gaps, volume imbalances, the full IFVG setup model, OB vs IFVG comparison, and the zone context (premium/discount) that makes each model high-probability.

---

### [[Market Structure - MOC]]
*The skeleton that tells you which direction is valid.*
Covers: swing highs/lows, break of structure, change of character, higher timeframe bias, premium/discount zones, and how order blocks are validated by the structural breaks they cause.

---

### [[Sessions & Timing - MOC]]
*When to watch, when to trade, when to do nothing.*
Covers: Asian session range, London open, NY open, kill zones, the Power of Three model, session reference levels, and SMT divergence at session opens. The same setup at the wrong time is a different setup.

---

### [[Risk & Psychology - MOC]]
*The infrastructure that keeps you in the game.*
Covers: the 1% rule, position sizing, stop loss placement, take profit targets, trade invalidation, the patience/discipline pair, pre-trade checklist, trade journaling, and backtesting. Edge without this is theoretical.

## The Core Loop

```
Read HTF Bias (4H/1D)
    ↓
Wait for Kill Zone (London or NY open)
    ↓
Watch for Liquidity Sweep of a key level
    ↓
Wait for Displacement → FVG or IFVG forms
    ↓
Run Confluences Checklist (score ≥ 4)
    ↓
Enter on zone retest + rejection candle
    ↓
SL beyond the zone · TP1 at internal liquidity · TP2 at external liquidity
    ↓
Log in Trade Journal
```
