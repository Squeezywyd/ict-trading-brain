# Psychology — Patience

## Definition
In ICT trading, patience is the **ability to do nothing until a high-quality setup appears**, and to hold a valid trade through normal noise without premature exit. It is the mental skill that makes the entire system work — because the system is built on waiting for specific, rare confluences. Impatience destroys the edge.

There are two forms of patience required:

1. **Pre-entry patience:** Waiting for all confluence conditions to align before entering
2. **In-trade patience:** Holding a position through natural retracement noise until the target is hit (or the trade is clearly invalidated)

## Rules

### Pre-Entry Patience
- There is no "good enough" setup — either it qualifies by the [[Confluences Checklist]] or it doesn't
- On any given day, the right answer may be **no trade** — this is a valid, disciplined outcome
- FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) is the enemy of patience — a setup you chased is usually a [[Worst Setups|worst setup]]
- The [[Kill Zones]] structure enforces patience by giving you defined windows to watch; outside them, do nothing

### In-Trade Patience
- Normal retracement into a [[Fair Value Gap]] or [[Order Block (Bullish)|OB]] does not require action — that is the expected behavior
- Only exit a live trade when the [[Trade Invalidation]] criteria are met — not because price is temporarily against you
- If the SL is correctly placed (beyond the sweep wick or zone boundary), trust it — small heat is expected

### Building Patience Practically
- Backtest 100+ setups — see how many non-qualifying "looks good" setups fail; this calibrates patience
- Write down the setup criteria before watching the chart — accountability to a pre-written standard removes in-the-moment rationalization
- Review the [[Trade Journal Template]] weekly — trades taken without full confluence will have lower win rates, proving patience pays

### The Patience Paradox
- The best setups are the ones you had to wait longest for — they usually form after the majority of retail traders have given up
- When a setup "feels obvious," it often means retail has already positioned and smart money will fade it
