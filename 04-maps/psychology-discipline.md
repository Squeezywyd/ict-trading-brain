# Psychology — Discipline

## Definition
Discipline in trading is the **ability to execute your rules exactly, every time, regardless of emotion or circumstance**. It is the bridge between knowledge and performance. You can know every ICT concept perfectly and still lose money without discipline — because trading rules are only valuable when followed in live conditions under pressure.

The primary discipline challenges in this system:

1. **Not trading outside [[Kill Zones]]** — even when you see "a really good setup"
2. **Honoring the stop loss** — not moving it away from you when the trade is uncomfortable
3. **Taking partial profit at TP1** — not holding for "just a bit more"
4. **Sizing at 1%** — not increasing size after a string of wins or to "make back" a loss
5. **Logging every trade** — including the ones you're embarrassed about

## Rules

### The Discipline Framework

**Rule 1 — Pre-commitment:** Write your plan before the session. If you plan to only trade London FVG setups with 4H bullish bias, that is the rule for the day. No exceptions.

**Rule 2 — No negotiation during live trades:** Once in a trade, you only have two actions: hold (if invalidation criteria are not met) or exit (if they are). Moving SL, changing TP, adding to a position — none of these are allowed.

**Rule 3 — Respect the daily loss limit:** After 2% daily drawdown, the session is over. No revenge trading. No "one more trade." Shut down the platform.

**Rule 4 — The 24-hour rule for review:** Never change your system rules during a live session or on the same day as a loss. Wait 24 hours. Most post-loss "improvements" are emotional reactions disguised as analysis.

**Rule 5 — Log rule breaks honestly:** In the [[Trade Journal Template]], the "rule breaks" field must be filled honestly. Logging a rule break is not punishment — it is the feedback mechanism that improves discipline over time.

### Discipline vs Stubbornness
- **Discipline** = holding a valid trade through normal noise because invalidation criteria are not met
- **Stubbornness** = holding a trade past the invalidation criteria because you "believe" in it
- If [[Trade Invalidation]] criteria are met, exit immediately — that is discipline, not defeat
