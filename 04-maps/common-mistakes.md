# Common Mistakes

## Definition
This note catalogs the **most frequent and most costly mistakes** made when applying ICT methodology. Every mistake here has been documented because it looks reasonable in the moment but consistently produces losses. Study this list before every session.

## Rules

### Mistake 1 — Trading Against HTF Bias
Entering a short when the 4H chart is in bullish structure, or a long in a bearish trend, without a confirmed [[Change of Character]]. The ICT system is a trend-following framework at its core — counter-trend trades require CHOCH + sweep confirmation.

### Mistake 2 — Trading Outside Kill Zones
Entering an [[Order Block (Bullish)|OB]] or [[Fair Value Gap]] at 3 PM EST because "it looks valid." FVGs and OBs form all day — only the ones touched during [[Kill Zones]] have institutional backing. Outside kill zones, retail players dominate = random outcomes.

### Mistake 3 — Entering on a Mitigated OB
Re-entering an [[Order Block (Bullish)|OB]] that already had its first clean retest. The orders are gone. The zone is coincidence. See [[Mitigation]] for why first-touch-only matters.

### Mistake 4 — Using Wicks as BOS/CHOCH Confirmation
Treating a wick below a [[Swing Low]] as a [[Break of Structure]]. ICT requires a **candle close** through the level — wicks do not count. Using wick-based BOS = false structure reads = wrong bias.

### Mistake 5 — Ignoring the Stop Hunt / Entering Before It Completes
Seeing price approaching a swing level and entering early — before the [[Liquidity Sweep]] occurs. The sweep is the signal, not the approach. Entering early means your SL is inside the sweep zone = stopped out at the exact worst point.

### Mistake 6 — Stacked FVG Confusion
When multiple [[Fair Value Gap|FVGs]] overlap in the same area, entering anyway. Overlapping FVGs = the market has visited and partially filled the imbalance multiple times = the zone is not clean. ICT rule: multiple FVGs = uncertainty — zoom out or avoid.

### Mistake 7 — Skipping the 1% Rule
Taking 3% risk on a "high confidence" trade. No setup is worth more than 1% — the market can print any candle at any time regardless of confluence. Breaking this rule once after a good run is how accounts blow up.

### Mistake 8 — Chasing Entry After Displacement
Entering at market after a [[Displacement Candle]] fires, instead of waiting for the [[Fair Value Gap]] retest. Chasing = poor R:R + entry at the top of the displacement = stopped by normal retracement.

### Mistake 9 — Moving SL Against the Trade
Widening the stop loss because the trade is uncomfortable. This converts a defined-risk trade into an undefined one. A valid SL placement is based on structure — discomfort is not a structural reason.

### Mistake 10 — Not Logging the Losing Trades
Skipping the [[Trade Journal Template]] on a losing day. Losses contain the most valuable data. Not logging them means repeating them.
