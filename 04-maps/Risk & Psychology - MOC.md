# Risk & Psychology - MOC

> **Purpose:** The infrastructure that keeps you in the game long enough for your edge to compound. All the ICT concepts in this vault are worthless if risk and psychology are broken — this MOC covers everything from position sizing to the mental habits that make rules stick.

---

## Risk Rules

The hard rules that govern every single trade regardless of setup quality.

- [[Risk Management]] — the 1% rule, position sizing formula, daily loss limits, R:R requirements
- [[Stop Loss Placement]] — exact SL location rules for FVG, IFVG, OB, and sweep-based entries
- [[Take Profit Rules]] — two-target model: TP1 at [[Internal Liquidity]], TP2 at [[External Liquidity]]
- [[Trade Invalidation]] — when to exit a live trade; accepting a loss is a risk management act
- [[Internal Liquidity]] — TP1 location; where 50% of the position is closed and SL moves to BE
- [[External Liquidity]] — TP2 location; where the remaining position is closed

---

## The Psychology Pair

The two complementary mental skills. Together they produce consistent execution.

- [[Psychology - Patience]] — waiting for qualified setups; the pre-entry mental skill
- [[Psychology - Discipline]] — executing rules exactly under pressure; the in-trade mental skill
- [[Common Mistakes]] — all 10 catalogued mistakes are ultimately discipline failures
- [[Worst Setups]] — worst setups are almost always entered due to impatience or FOMO

## Process & Accountability

The systems that make rules self-enforcing over time.

- [[Pre-Trade Checklist]] — the daily routine that structures patience before the session begins
- [[Confluences Checklist]] — the real-time scoring system that enforces entry quality standards
- [[Trade Journal Template]] — the logging framework that turns every trade into feedback
- [[Backtesting Rules]] — the process that proves your edge and calibrates your confidence

---

## The Feedback Loop

How the system improves itself.

- [[Backtesting Rules]] → reveals which setups have edge → builds conviction under drawdown
- [[Trade Journal Template]] → exposes repeated rule breaks → targets discipline improvements
- [[Common Mistakes]] → the pattern library of known errors → pre-empts them in real time
- [[Best Setups]] → studying what works reinforces patience for A+ setups only
