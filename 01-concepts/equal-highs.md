# Equal Highs

## Definition
Equal Highs (EQH) are **two or more swing highs at approximately the same price level**. Retail traders see this pattern as a "double top" or "triple top" and short it. This clustering of retail shorts above the highs creates a dense pool of [[Buy-Side Liquidity]] — stop-loss orders stacked at the same level.

ICT does not short equal highs — it uses them as a *target* in bullish context, or a *signal* that a liquidity sweep is imminent.

*Example:* NQ prints a swing high at 17,820, then another high at 17,818. These are equal highs. Retail traders short both and place stops at 17,835. [[Buy-Side Liquidity]] is stacked at 17,835. A sweep to 17,838 followed by a reversal = textbook liquidity sweep off equal highs.

## Rules
- Equal highs within 5–10 pips/points are treated as the same level — they don't need to be exact
- Equal highs are a **target**, not a short signal — price is drawn toward them first
- After a sweep of equal highs, look for a bearish [[Displacement Candle]] and [[Fair Value Gap]] for the short entry
- In a bearish trend, a sweep of equal highs = the manipulation phase — the subsequent short is the trade
- Equal highs form on all timeframes — higher TF equal highs are more significant targets
- Back-to-back equal highs being swept = very reliable signal (retail has placed large stop clusters)
