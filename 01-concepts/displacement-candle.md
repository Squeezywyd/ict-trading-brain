# Displacement Candle

## Definition
A Displacement Candle is a **large, decisive candle** that moves price aggressively in one direction, creating [[Imbalance]] in its wake. It is the hallmark of institutional order flow entering the market — normal price action does not produce displacement; only large market orders do.

Displacement is the engine behind [[Fair Value Gap]] creation, [[Break of Structure]], and [[IFVG]] formation. Without displacement, a candle is "noise" — with displacement, it signals intent.

**Visual characteristics:**
- Large body relative to recent candles (3–5× the average candle size)
- Small wicks relative to body
- Closes near the high (bullish) or low (bearish)
- Often creates a gap between its close and the next candle open

*Example:* During London open, a 1-minute candle on NQ travels 80 points in a single bar. This is displacement. The prior candle's wick and the following candle's wick do not overlap — an FVG has been created in that 80-point range.

## Rules
- A displacement candle must create an FVG (no overlap between candle 1 wick and candle 3 wick)
- Displacement during a [[Kill Zones|kill zone]] = institutional; displacement at 3 PM EST = likely news or low-liquidity noise
- A displacement that closes beyond a [[Swing High]] or [[Swing Low]] = [[Break of Structure]] confirmed
- A displacement that closes fully through a [[Fair Value Gap]] = [[IFVG]] confirmed
- Do not enter on the displacement candle itself — wait for price to retrace into the FVG it created
- The larger the displacement relative to recent candles, the more significant the imbalance
