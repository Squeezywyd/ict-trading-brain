# Session Highs/Lows

## Definition
Session Highs and Lows are the **highest and lowest prices traded during each trading session** (Asian, London, NY). They serve as critical reference points for the next session — they mark where [[Buy-Side Liquidity]] and [[Sell-Side Liquidity]] are pooled, and they define the range that the next session's kill zone will likely target.

**Key session levels to mark:**
- **Previous day high/low (PDH/PDL)** — the most important, swept constantly
- **Asian session high/low** — targeted by London open
- **London session high/low** — targeted by NY open or the next day
- **Weekly high/low** — macro liquidity pool for swing trades

*Example:* Yesterday's high on NQ was 17,850. Today, at London open, price spikes to 17,860, triggering BSL above the previous day's high, then reverses hard. The PDH was the session high acting as a liquidity target.

## Rules
- Mark the previous day high and low on your chart **before** each trading session
- The PDH and PDL are the two most important same-session liquidity levels
- A session high that gets swept and closed below = the session bias is shifting bearish
- A session low that gets swept and closed above = the session bias is shifting bullish
- Do not trade against the direction of a cleared session high/low without a structural reason
- Session highs/lows on weekly and monthly TF = major external liquidity levels
