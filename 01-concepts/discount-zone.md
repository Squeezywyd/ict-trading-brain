# Discount Zone

## Definition
The Discount Zone is the **lower 50% of a price range** — from the range low up to the 50% midpoint (equilibrium). Price in discount is considered "cheap" relative to the current range. Smart money **buys** in discount and avoids longs in premium.

*Example:* Swing low at 4,400, swing high at 4,600. Range = 200 points. Equilibrium = 4,500. Discount zone = 4,400–4,500. If price retraces to 4,450 in a bullish trend, you're in discount — prime location for a long entry.

## Rules
- In a bullish trend, look for long entries when price retraces into discount (below the 50% level)
- The 61.8%–79% retracement level (OTE zone) inside discount is the **optimal** long entry area
- A [[Order Block (Bullish)]] sitting inside the discount zone = ideal long entry confluence
- A bullish [[IFVG]] in discount = highest probability long setup
- Never enter shorts in discount when [[Higher Timeframe Bias]] is bullish — you're fighting the trend
- Discount + bullish HTF bias + [[Kill Zones|kill zone]] timing = optimal long setup
- Measure discount fresh for every new swing high/low pair — stale ranges lose relevance
