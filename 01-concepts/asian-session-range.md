# Asian Session Range

## Definition
The Asian session runs **20:00–00:00 EST** (01:00–05:00 GMT). During this window, forex pairs (especially those not involving JPY) consolidate into a tight range as volume is low. ICT methodology treats this range as the **accumulation phase** — institutions are quietly positioning before the London open.

The Asian session's high and low are the two critical levels: one will be swept by [[London Open]], and the other becomes the directional target.

*Example:* EURUSD Asian session: high = 1.0860, low = 1.0830. At London open (08:00 GMT), price drops to 1.0825 (sweeps the low). Bullish reversal. The target is the Asian high at 1.0860, then beyond.

## Rules
- Mark the Asian session high and low at **exactly** the end of the Asian session (00:00 EST) before going to sleep
- These two levels are the most likely targets for the London open [[Liquidity Sweep]]
- If London sweeps the Asian low → look for a bullish setup; if it sweeps the Asian high → look for a bearish setup
- A sweep of both sides (fakeout in both directions) is possible but rare — it signals a very choppy day
- Asian session for indices (NQ, ES, SPX) is less relevant — focus on the overnight session high/low instead
- The Asian range tends to be tighter in currency pairs with low Asian liquidity (EURUSD, GBPUSD) and wider for JPY pairs
- After marking the range, wait — do not trade during Asian session unless you have a very specific setup
