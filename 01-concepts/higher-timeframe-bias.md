# Higher Timeframe Bias

## Definition
Higher Timeframe (HTF) Bias is the **directional context from the 4H or 1D chart** that dictates which direction is valid for lower-timeframe entries. It is the foundational step of every ICT trade — all setups must align with the HTF bias, or they are counter-trend trades with significantly lower probability.

**Top-down analysis:**
1. Start at 1D or 4H: determine trend direction (bullish or bearish [[Market Structure]])
2. Drop to 1H or 15M: find the kill zone setup aligned with HTF direction
3. Execute on 5M or 1M: pinpoint entry using [[Fair Value Gap]] or [[IFVG]]

*Example:* 4H chart shows bullish structure — HH at 4,800, making an HL at 4,650. HTF bias = bullish. On the 15M, at London open, price sweeps the HL low, creates a bullish FVG. Enter long. The HTF bias confirmed this was the right direction.

## Rules
- **Always determine 4H or 1D bias before anything else** — this is non-negotiable
- Only take longs above a bullish [[Break of Structure]] on the HTF; only take shorts below a bearish BOS
- A [[Change of Character]] on 4H signals that the bias may be flipping — re-evaluate before the next trade
- Mark 4H swing highs and lows and update them each session
- If 1D and 4H disagree, go with 1D — always defer to the higher timeframe when uncertain
- Never enter against the HTF bias without a clear CHOCH + [[Liquidity Sweep]] on the HTF
- Weekly bias > daily bias > 4H bias — when multiple align in the same direction, confidence is maximum
