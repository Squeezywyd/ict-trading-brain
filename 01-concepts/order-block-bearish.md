# Order Block (Bearish)

## Definition
A Bearish Order Block is the **last bullish candle** (or last up-close candle) directly before a strong, impulsive downward move. It marks the zone where institutional sell orders were placed — the final "distribution" candle before the sell-off.

Institutions distribute their long positions (or build shorts) in that bullish candle, using the upward momentum to get better prices on their sells. When price rallies back into that zone, institutions defend it again by selling.

*Example:* On the 4H EURUSD chart, a bullish candle ranges from 1.0830–1.0870, followed by a sharp 3-candle drop to 1.0720. The 1.0830–1.0870 zone is the bearish OB. When price retraces to 1.0850, it's a high-probability short.

## Rules
- Must meet **all four criteria**: swept [[Buy-Side Liquidity]], created [[Imbalance]], remained untouched, caused a [[Break of Structure]] to the downside
- Enter on the **first retest only** — once price fully closes above the OB high, it is mitigated
- OB zone = high to low of the last bullish candle before the bearish move
- Look for a bearish rejection wick or bearish engulfing when price enters the OB
- Best setups form at [[Premium Zone]] levels — if the OB sits in premium and HTF is bearish, it's high confluence
- Stop loss: above the high of the OB candle — see [[Stop Loss Placement]]
- The bearish OB preceding an [[IFVG]] makes for an extremely high-probability combined zone
