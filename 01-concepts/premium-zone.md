# Premium Zone

## Definition
The Premium Zone is the **upper 50% of a price range** — from the 50% midpoint (equilibrium) up to the range high. In ICT methodology, premium is where price is considered "expensive" relative to the range. Smart money **sells** in premium and **buys** in discount.

Price ranges are measured between the most recent significant swing low and swing high on the working timeframe.

*Example:* Swing low at 17,000, swing high at 17,400. The range is 400 points. Equilibrium = 17,200 (50%). Premium zone = 17,200–17,400. If price is at 17,350 and you're looking for shorts, you're trading in premium — correct.

## Rules
- In a bearish trend, look for short entries when price retraces into premium (50%–79% of the range)
- In a bullish trend, never enter longs in premium — wait for price to pull back to [[Discount Zone]]
- A [[Order Block (Bearish)|Bearish Order Block]] sitting inside the premium zone = ideal short entry confluence
- An [[IFVG]] in premium zone (bearish) = highest probability short setup
- The 61.8%–79% of the range (the "optimal trade entry" or OTE zone) is inside premium — this is the sweet spot for bearish entries
- Selling in discount and buying in premium are the most common [[Common Mistakes]] of retail traders
- Premium + [[Higher Timeframe Bias]] bearish + [[Kill Zones|kill zone]] timing = optimal short setup
