# Swing High

## Definition
A Swing High is a **price peak where the highs of both the candles to the left and right are lower**. It represents a point where price failed to continue higher and reversed. In ICT, swing highs are the foundational reference points for [[Market Structure]], liquidity pools, and target levels.

**Simple rule:** A candle is a swing high if both its neighboring candles have lower highs.

*Example:* On the 1H chart, candle sequence highs: 4520, 4545, 4530. The middle candle at 4545 is a swing high — it has lower candles on both sides.

## Rules
- Mark swing highs on your working timeframe AND the [[Higher Timeframe Bias|higher timeframe]] (4H/1D)
- Swing highs above price = [[Buy-Side Liquidity]] — stops from shorts cluster just above them
- A swing high that is broken with a full candle close = [[Break of Structure]] to the upside
- **Equal Highs** (two or more swing highs at the same price) = strong [[Buy-Side Liquidity]] pool
- A swing high that forms after a sweep of a [[Swing Low]] = the beginning of a new bullish leg
- Higher highs (HH) in sequence = bullish [[Market Structure]]; lower highs (LH) = bearish structure
- Major swing highs on 4H/1D = [[External Liquidity]] targets for large institutional moves

## Connexions
- [[Swing Low]] — the mirror; swing highs and lows together define market structure
- [[Market Structure]] — swing highs define the peaks that describe trend direction
- [[Buy-Side Liquidity]] — stops cluster just above swing highs; they are BSL pools
- [[Break of Structure]] — a close above a swing high is a bullish BOS
- [[Equal Highs]] — two or more swing highs at the same level = premium liquidity target
- [[External Liquidity]] — major swing highs beyond the current range = external BSL
- [[Higher Timeframe Bias]] — HTF swing highs are the macro reference points for direction
- [[Draw on Liquidity]] — bullish draw always aims at the next swing high (BSL)
- [[Liquidity Sweep]] — swing highs are the primary target for upward liquidity sweeps
- [[Stop Hunt]] — retail stops above swing highs are the prey; the stop hunt targets them
- [[Change of Character]] — a CHOCH is triggered when price breaks below the most recent swing high (bearish)
- [[Order Block (Bearish)]] — bearish OBs typically form at or just below a recent swing high
- [[London Open]] — London frequently sweeps the prior session's swing high for BSL
- [[Premium Zone]] — the area around a swing high is in premium territory; it's where shorts are valid
