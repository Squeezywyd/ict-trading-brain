# Order Block (Bullish)

## Definition
A Bullish Order Block (OB) is the **last bearish candle** (or last down-close candle) directly before a strong, impulsive upward move. It represents the zone where institutional buy orders were placed — the final "loading" candle before the launch.

The logic: institutions accumulate long positions in that bearish candle. They push price down briefly (the OB candle) to fill their orders at a better price, then release the move upward. When price returns to that zone, those same institutions defend their position.

*Example:* On the 1H NQ chart, there's a bearish candle from 17,100–17,050, followed by a strong 3-candle rally to 17,300. That 17,050–17,100 zone is the bullish OB. When price retraces back to 17,080, it's a high-probability long.

## Rules
- Must meet **all four criteria**: swept liquidity, created [[Imbalance]] (FVG), remained untouched since, caused a [[Break of Structure]]
- Enter on the **first retest only** — once price closes through the OB, it is mitigated and no longer valid
- The OB zone is the range of the candle: high to low of the last bearish candle
- Look for a rejection wick or engulfing candle when price enters the OB zone
- Best OBs form during [[Kill Zones]] — especially during [[London Open]] or [[NY Open]]
- The OB must be preceded by a [[Liquidity Sweep]] — without a sweep, it's a low-quality OB
- Combine with a [[Fair Value Gap]] inside the OB zone for maximum confluence
- Stop loss: below the low of the OB candle — see [[Stop Loss Placement]]
