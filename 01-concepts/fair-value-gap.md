# Fair Value Gap

## Definition
A Fair Value Gap (FVG) is a **3-candle imbalance pattern** where price moves so aggressively that a gap forms between candle 1's wick and candle 3's wick. That gap is an area where buy and sell orders were never matched — price skipped through too fast.

**Bullish FVG:** Gap between the *high of candle 1* and the *low of candle 3* during an upward move.
**Bearish FVG:** Gap between the *low of candle 1* and the *high of candle 3* during a downward move.

**Key rule:** If candle 1 and candle 3 wicks overlap even slightly, there is no FVG.

*Example:* On a 15M chart, price is in a bullish run. Candle 1 closes at 4500, candle 2 is a large bullish engulf, candle 3 opens at 4520. The gap between 4500 and 4520 is the FVG zone — price will likely revisit it before pushing higher.

## Rules
- Draw the box from candle 1 wick to candle 3 wick
- Enter when price retests the FVG and shows rejection (pin bar or engulfing candle inside the zone)
- A bullish FVG acts as support on retest; a bearish FVG acts as resistance
- **No wicks overlap = high urgency move** = stronger, more valid FVG
- If price closes fully beyond the opposite side of the FVG, it becomes an [[IFVG]] — the original gap is inverted
- Multiple overlapping FVGs on the same TF = uncertainty — avoid entries or zoom to [[Higher Timeframe Bias|HTF]]
- Best FVGs form after a [[Displacement Candle]] inside a [[Kill Zones|kill zone]]
- Stack with an [[Order Block (Bullish)]] or [[Order Block (Bearish)]] directly below/above for high-confluence entries
