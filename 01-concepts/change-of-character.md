# Change of Character

## Definition
A Change of Character (CHOCH) is a **structural break against the prevailing trend** on a lower timeframe — a close below a Higher Low in an uptrend, or a close above a Lower High in a downtrend. It is the first sign that the trend may be reversing.

A CHOCH does **not** confirm a reversal alone — it raises the alert. Only when a CHOCH on the lower TF aligns with a sweep of a major liquidity level is it tradeable.

**BOS vs CHOCH:**
- [[Break of Structure]] = continues the trend (HH in uptrend, LL in downtrend)
- CHOCH = breaks against the trend (close below HL in uptrend = bearish CHOCH)

*Example:* 1H chart shows an uptrend. Price makes a HL at 17,200. A bearish candle closes at 17,150 — below the HL. That's a bearish CHOCH on the 1H. Drop to the 15M: if a small BOS to the downside forms, the reversal has more weight.

## Rules
- CHOCH on lower TF (15M/1H) inside a higher TF retracement is an entry signal, not just a warning
- Wait for CHOCH + [[Liquidity Sweep]] of the nearest swing point for the highest probability reversal
- A CHOCH without a sweep is risky — price may just be consolidating
- After a CHOCH, wait for a retest of the broken level (now flipped) before entering — similar to a [[Breaker Block]] entry
- A CHOCH that aligns with a [[Fair Value Gap]] or [[IFVG]] zone is high confluence
- Multiple CHOCHes in the same direction = progressive structure shift = stronger reversal signal
- Never counter-trade a [[Higher Timeframe Bias]] CHOCH without extreme confirmation
