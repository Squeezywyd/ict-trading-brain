# Draw on Liquidity

## Definition
The Draw on Liquidity (DOL) is the **next significant liquidity pool** that price is most likely to be drawn toward. It is the magnetic target that explains why price is moving in a given direction — price is always seeking the next available pool of resting orders.

Understanding the DOL prevents you from taking entries that are "in the way" of price rather than aligned with it. Always ask: *Where is price going before it gets to my entry target?*

*Example:* On a 4H chart, there's [[Buy-Side Liquidity]] at 17,600 (a prior swing high). Price is at 17,200 trending upward. The DOL is 17,600 — price is drawn to it. Entries below 17,600 are valid; entries above would be fighting the DOL.

## Rules
- Always identify the DOL before entering a trade — it defines your take-profit target
- DOL is typically the nearest unswept [[Buy-Side Liquidity]] (for bullish) or [[Sell-Side Liquidity]] (for bearish)
- [[External Liquidity]] (beyond the current range) is the final DOL; [[Internal Liquidity]] is an intermediate stop
- Price respects intermediate internal liquidity before reaching external DOL — this is where partial profits are taken
- If your entry is between price and the DOL, the trade has room to run; if the DOL is between price and your entry, you're likely to be stopped
- The DOL shifts after it is swept — immediately reassess where the next pool is
- Match the DOL on the trading TF with the HTF draw for high-probability alignment

- [[External Liquidity]] — the final DOL beyond the current swing highs/lows
- [[Buy-Side Liquidity]] — BSL above price is the DOL in a bullish trend
- [[Sell-Side Liquidity]] — SSL below price is the DOL in a bearish trend
- [[Take Profit Rules]] — the DOL is the primary basis for setting take-profit levels
- [[Higher Timeframe Bias]] — the HTF DOL (4H or 1D liquidity pool) is the macro target
- [[Market Structure]] — structural swing points mark the locations of key DOL pools
- [[Power of Three]] — in PO3 model, the distribution phase drives price to the DOL
- [[Session Highs/Lows]] — prior session highs and lows are the most common intraday DOL targets
- [[Equal Highs]] — equal highs above price are the clearest, most visual DOL for a bullish move
- [[Equal Lows]] — equal lows below price are the clearest, most visual DOL for a bearish move
- [[Kill Zones]] — the DOL is typically reached during a kill zone; plan your TP timing around this
- [[Break of Structure]] — the DOL is usually aligned with the next structural break point
- [[Swing High]] — swing highs above the current range = the bullish external DOL on the macro level
- [[Swing Low]] — swing lows below the current range = the bearish external DOL on the macro level
