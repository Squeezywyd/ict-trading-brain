# Kill Zones

## Definition
Kill Zones are **specific time windows** during which institutional order flow is at its highest, producing the most reliable ICT setups. Trading outside kill zones dramatically reduces probability — the "same" setup inside a kill zone is a different, higher-quality setup than the same pattern at 3 PM EST.

**The three primary kill zones:**
1. **Asian session:** 20:00–00:00 EST — range building, liquidity accumulation
2. **London open:** 02:00–05:00 EST (= 08:00–11:00 GMT) — highest probability sweeps and reversals
3. **NY open:** 08:30–11:00 EST (= 13:30–16:00 GMT) — big directional moves, especially around news

## Rules
- **Only enter trades inside kill zones** — setups outside these windows are low probability
- The [[London Open]] is historically the most reliable for [[Liquidity Sweep]] → reversal setups
- The [[NY Open]] produces the biggest directional moves and is best for trend continuation
- The [[Asian Session Range]] defines the highs and lows that London will typically sweep
- Kill zone timing is fixed to clock time — use GMT for London, EST for NY to stay accurate
- A [[Fair Value Gap]] or [[IFVG]] formed inside a kill zone is significantly stronger than one formed midday
- News events (NFP, FOMC, CPI) supercharge the NY kill zone — setups around news are highest probability
- If you miss the kill zone entry, **wait for the next one** — do not chase outside the window
