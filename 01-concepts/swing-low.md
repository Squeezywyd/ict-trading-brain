# Swing Low

## Definition
A Swing Low is a **price trough where the lows of both neighboring candles are higher**. It represents a point where price failed to continue lower and reversed. Swing lows are foundational reference points for [[Market Structure]], [[Sell-Side Liquidity]] pools, and entry zones.

**Simple rule:** A candle is a swing low if both its neighboring candles have higher lows.

*Example:* On the 1H chart, candle lows: 4460, 4435, 4455. The middle candle at 4435 is a swing low — it has higher lows on both sides.

## Rules
- Mark swing lows on your working timeframe AND the [[Higher Timeframe Bias|higher timeframe]]
- Swing lows below price = [[Sell-Side Liquidity]] — stops from longs cluster just below them
- A swing low that is broken with a full candle close = [[Break of Structure]] to the downside
- **Equal Lows** (two or more swing lows at the same price) = strong [[Sell-Side Liquidity]] pool
- A swing low that forms after a sweep of a [[Swing High]] = the beginning of a new bearish leg
- Higher lows (HL) in sequence = bullish [[Market Structure]]; lower lows (LL) = bearish structure
- Major swing lows on 4H/1D = [[External Liquidity]] targets for large bearish institutional moves

## Connexions
- [[Swing High]] — the mirror; swing highs and lows together define market structure
- [[Market Structure]] — swing lows define the troughs that describe trend direction
- [[Sell-Side Liquidity]] — stops cluster just below swing lows; they are SSL pools
- [[Break of Structure]] — a close below a swing low is a bearish BOS
- [[Equal Lows]] — two or more swing lows at the same level = premium SSL target
- [[External Liquidity]] — major swing lows beyond the current range = external SSL
- [[Discount Zone]] — the area around a swing low is typically in discount; ideal for long entries
- [[Stop Loss Placement]] — stop losses on longs are placed just below swing lows
- [[Liquidity Sweep]] — swing lows are the primary target for downward liquidity sweeps
- [[Stop Hunt]] — retail stops below swing lows are the prey; the stop hunt targets them
- [[Change of Character]] — a bullish CHOCH is triggered when price closes back above a swept swing low
- [[Order Block (Bullish)]] — bullish OBs typically form at or just above a recent swing low
- [[Fair Value Gap]] — bullish FVGs often form after a swing low is swept and price reverses
- [[Internal Liquidity]] — minor swing lows within the current range mark internal SSL (TP1 for shorts)
