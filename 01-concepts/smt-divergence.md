# SMT Divergence

## Definition
SMT (Smart Money Technique) Divergence is a **confirmation signal** that compares two correlated instruments. When a correlated pair makes a new swing high/low but the other **fails to confirm** it (does not make a new high/low), the divergence signals institutional intent — the stronger instrument will pull the weaker one.

**Key correlated pairs for SMT:**
- Indices: NQ (Nasdaq) vs ES (S&P 500) — both should move together
- Forex: EURUSD vs GBPUSD — highly correlated; divergence is meaningful
- Gold vs DXY — inverse correlation; if they both go up simultaneously, one is lying

*Example:* ES makes a new high at 5,220. NQ fails to make a new high — it only reaches 17,900 vs its prior high of 17,950. This NQ weakness while ES is at a new high = bearish SMT divergence. A [[Liquidity Sweep]] + reversal from ES is likely.

## Rules
- Both instruments must be correlated — SMT only works between instruments that normally move together
- SMT divergence works on the **same timeframe** — compare the same candle closes across both
- A new high in one + failed high in other = **bearish SMT** (bias short)
- A new low in one + failed low in other = **bullish SMT** (bias long)
- SMT alone is not an entry — it is a filter/confirmation for a setup already in progress
- Highest value when SMT occurs at a [[Kill Zones|kill zone]] alongside a [[Liquidity Sweep]] or [[IFVG]]
- An [[IFVG]] setup combined with SMT divergence is the highest probability short/long in ICT methodology
