# Equal Lows

## Definition
Equal Lows (EQL) are **two or more swing lows at approximately the same price level**. Retail traders see this as a "double bottom" or "triple bottom" and go long there, placing stop-losses just below. This creates a stacked [[Sell-Side Liquidity]] pool — dense stop clusters at a predictable level.

ICT uses equal lows as a *target* in bearish context (draw on liquidity) or as the *trigger zone* for a bullish reversal after the sweep.

*Example:* EURUSD prints lows at 1.0800 and 1.0802. Retail goes long and places stops at 1.0790. A sudden drop to 1.0787 sweeps those stops (equal lows swept), then price rockets to 1.0870. The sweep of equal lows triggered the bullish distribution.

## Rules
- Equal lows within 5–10 pips/points of each other are treated as the same level
- Equal lows are a **draw on liquidity target** in bearish context — not a buy signal until swept
- After a sweep of equal lows, look for a bullish [[Displacement Candle]] and [[Fair Value Gap]] for the long entry
- In a bullish trend, a sweep of equal lows = the manipulation phase — the long is the trade
- Multiple equal lows tested over time = increasingly powerful SSL pool (retail keeps adding stops)
- Timeframe matters: 1D equal lows swept = macro bullish signal; 5M equal lows swept = intraday signal
