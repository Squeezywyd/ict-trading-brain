# London Open

## Definition
The London Open kill zone runs **08:00–11:00 GMT** (02:00–05:00 EST). It is historically the **highest probability time window** for [[Liquidity Sweep]] → reversal setups. The majority of the world's forex volume enters during this window, and institutions use the pre-London illiquid period to push price into sweep territory, then reverse hard at open.

The classic London open pattern:
1. **Asian session** builds a range (high and low marked)
2. During early London (08:00–09:00 GMT), price sweeps one side of the Asian range
3. Price reverses sharply after the sweep and runs in the opposite direction

*Example:* GBPUSD Asian session high at 1.2650. At 08:05 GMT, price spikes to 1.2662, triggering buy-stops. By 08:15, price has reversed to 1.2620. The London sweep and reversal — textbook.

## Rules
- Mark the [[Asian Session Range]] high and low before London opens
- At London open, observe which side of the range price targets first — that is the sweep
- A sweep of the Asian low during London = bullish setup; sweep of Asian high = bearish setup
- Only enter after the sweep is complete — look for a [[Displacement Candle]] and [[Fair Value Gap]]
- The window 08:00–09:30 GMT is the highest probability — after 10:00 GMT, quality decays
- Do not trade the London-NY overlap (12:00–13:00 GMT) — liquidity is thin and choppy
- Combine London sweep with a [[Higher Timeframe Bias]] filter — only take London sweeps that align with HTF direction
