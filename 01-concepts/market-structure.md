# Market Structure

## Definition
Market structure is the framework of **swing highs and swing lows** that describe whether the market is trending, ranging, or reversing. It is the first thing to determine before any trade — all ICT entries are taken in the direction of the prevailing structure on the relevant timeframe.

**Bullish structure (uptrend):** Higher highs (HH) and higher lows (HL) — price is making progressively higher swing points.
**Bearish structure (downtrend):** Lower highs (LH) and lower lows (LL) — price is making progressively lower swing points.
**Ranging:** Price alternates between equal highs and equal lows without clear directional bias.

*Example:* On the 4H chart, NQ prints: HL at 17,000 → HH at 17,400 → HL at 17,150 → HH at 17,600. This is clean bullish structure. Only take longs.

## Rules
- **Always determine [[Higher Timeframe Bias]] first** — 4H or 1D structure dictates which direction is valid
- Mark all significant swing highs and swing lows on your working TF
- In bullish structure: only trade longs, entering at HLs (= [[Discount Zone]])
- In bearish structure: only trade shorts, entering at LHs (= [[Premium Zone]])
- A [[Break of Structure]] is when price closes beyond a key swing point — structure has changed
- A [[Change of Character]] is a lower-TF structural shift that warns of a potential higher-TF reversal
- Do not trade against structure; fading a trend without a clear CHOCH is a [[Common Mistakes|common mistake]]
- Structure must be evaluated at multiple timeframes — 4H for bias, 15M for entries
