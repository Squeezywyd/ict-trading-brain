# Imbalance

## Definition
An Imbalance is any area on the chart where **price moved so fast that buy and sell orders were not matched**. It is the parent concept that [[Fair Value Gap]] and [[Volume Imbalance]] are specific forms of. Markets are constantly trying to "balance" — when price is imbalanced, the market gravitates back to fill the gap.

Imbalances are caused by [[Displacement Candle|displacement candles]] — institutional market orders entering with enough size to push price past the available resting limit orders.

*Example:* Price is trading at 4,500. A news event causes a burst of buying. Price jumps from 4,500 to 4,540 in one candle. Between 4,500 and 4,540, no price discovery occurred — orders were not matched. This is an imbalance. Price will likely retrace into 4,500–4,540 later.

## Rules
- Imbalances act as **magnets** — price tends to retrace into them before continuing
- A [[Fair Value Gap]] is a specifically structured imbalance with no overlap between candle wicks
- A [[Volume Imbalance]] is the gap between a displacement close and the next candle open (no wick overlap)
- Do not use imbalances alone as entries — combine with [[Market Structure]] alignment and [[Kill Zones]] timing
- Not all imbalances are equal: imbalances created during [[Kill Zones]] are more reliable than midday gaps
- Multiple imbalances stacked in the same zone = very strong magnetic area
- After an imbalance is fully "filled" (price trades through the entire zone), it loses its magnetic pull

## Connexions
- [[Fair Value Gap]] — the most structured and tradeable form of imbalance
- [[Volume Imbalance]] — a related imbalance type; the gap between a close and next open
- [[Displacement Candle]] — the cause of imbalance; displacement creates all FVGs and VIs
- [[IFVG]] — an inverted imbalance; the zone flips from support to resistance or vice versa
- [[Order Block (Bullish)]] — OBs often align with adjacent imbalances for stacked confluence
- [[Kill Zones]] — imbalances formed during kill zones are the only ones worth trading
- [[Market Structure]] — only trade imbalances that align with the prevailing structure
- [[Confluences Checklist]] — imbalance in the right zone + structure + session = A+ setup
- [[Liquidity Sweep]] — imbalances most commonly form immediately after a liquidity sweep
- [[Break of Structure]] — a BOS creates a strong imbalance zone that price tends to revisit
- [[Power of Three]] — the distribution phase of PO3 is driven by displacement and leaves imbalances behind
- [[Higher Timeframe Bias]] — HTF imbalances carry more weight than LTF imbalances; align them
- [[London Open]] — London creates the most significant intraday imbalances; prioritize those formed here
- [[Breaker Block]] — a broken OB has an embedded imbalance; the two concepts overlap at the same level
