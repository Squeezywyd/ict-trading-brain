# Volume Imbalance

## Definition
A Volume Imbalance (VI) is the **gap between one candle's close and the next candle's open** when price jumps aggressively. Unlike a [[Fair Value Gap]] which spans 3 candles, a volume imbalance is a 2-candle phenomenon — the close of candle 1 and the open of candle 2 don't overlap.

Volume imbalances are slightly less strong than FVGs because they represent a gap in only one direction (the opening gap), while FVGs show that price ran through an entire zone without filling orders.

*Example:* Candle 1 closes at 4,520. Candle 2 opens at 4,535. The gap from 4,520 to 4,535 is a volume imbalance — no trading occurred in that range. Price tends to retrace into this zone.

## Rules
- A volume imbalance is valid when the close of candle 1 and the open of candle 2 have no overlap
- Like an FVG, price tends to retrace into a VI before continuing — use it as a retracement entry zone
- VIs form during news events, gap openings, or session opens — these are the most reliable
- A VI that forms at the same level as an FVG = very strong imbalance zone (stack them)
- Volume imbalances at session opens (especially [[NY Open]]) are particularly reliable
- Do not treat a VI as equal to an FVG — VIs are lower priority; prefer FVGs when both exist nearby
