# Power of Three (PO3)

## Definition
The Power of Three (PO3) is ICT's core **session-based market model**. It describes how institutional money orchestrates price movement across the three main trading sessions into a predictable three-phase cycle:

1. **Accumulation** (Asian session, 20:00–00:00 EST): Institutions quietly build positions in a tight range. Price is compressed.
2. **Manipulation** (London open, 02:00–05:00 EST): Price fakes out in the *opposite* direction of the true move — a [[Liquidity Sweep]] runs stops from retail traders who entered during Asian.
3. **Distribution** (NY open, 08:30–11:00 EST): The true, institutional direction unfolds. Price moves decisively toward the [[Draw on Liquidity]].

*Example:* EUR/USD Asian session builds a range (1.0830–1.0860). At London open, price drops to 1.0820 — sweeping the Asian low, triggering retail stop-losses. Then at NY open (13:30 GMT), price surges to 1.0920, completing the true bullish distribution phase.

## Rules
- Once you identify the likely PO3 direction (from [[Higher Timeframe Bias]]), the manipulation phase is your entry trigger
- The manipulation phase (sweep) should be against the daily bias — if HTF is bullish, expect a bearish sweep first
- Enter during or immediately after the manipulation phase — not during distribution
- The distribution target = the nearest [[External Liquidity]] or next major swing point
- The model repeats daily, weekly, and monthly — zoom out to see higher-TF PO3 cycles
- The manipulation phase is where most retail traders get stopped out — your edge is knowing this phase is engineered
