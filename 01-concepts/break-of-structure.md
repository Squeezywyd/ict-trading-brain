# Break of Structure

## Definition
A Break of Structure (BOS) occurs when price **closes beyond a key swing point** in the direction of the prevailing trend — a higher high closes above the previous HH in an uptrend, or a lower low closes below the previous LL in a downtrend.

A BOS **confirms** the existing trend; it is continuation, not reversal. It means smart money is still in control of that direction.

**Distinguish from CHOCH:** A BOS happens *with* the trend; a [[Change of Character]] happens *against* the trend and signals potential reversal.

*Example:* In a bullish trend, price makes HH at 17,400. Price dips to 17,200 (HL), then rallies to close at 17,450. That close above 17,400 is a BOS — bullish structure confirmed, continue looking for longs.

## Rules
- A BOS requires a **full candle close** beyond the swing point — a wick is not a BOS
- In an uptrend: only take longs above a bullish BOS; in a downtrend: only take shorts below a bearish BOS
- After a BOS, price often retraces to fill a nearby [[Fair Value Gap]] or test an [[Order Block (Bullish)]] before continuing
- A BOS also validates the [[Order Block (Bullish)]] or [[Order Block (Bearish)]] that caused it
- BOS on [[Higher Timeframe Bias|higher TF]] = strong continuation; BOS only on lower TF = weaker, may be noise
- Multiple consecutive BOSes in the same direction = strong trend momentum
- A BOS followed immediately by a CHOCH is called a "shift" — structure is becoming unstable

## Connexions
- [[Change of Character]] — the opposite event; CHOCH goes *against* the trend, BOS goes *with* it
- [[Market Structure]] — BOS is the mechanism by which market structure is maintained
- [[Higher Timeframe Bias]] — HTF BOS is the primary filter for trade direction
- [[Order Block (Bullish)]] — OBs are validated by the BOS they cause; the BOS is proof of institutional activity
- [[Fair Value Gap]] — after a BOS, the FVG created by the displacement is the retracement entry zone
- [[Displacement Candle]] — BOSes are usually created by a displacement candle
- [[Internal Liquidity]] — a BOS often sweeps internal liquidity in the process
- [[Swing High]] — a BOS occurs when price closes beyond a swing high (bullish) or swing low (bearish)
- [[Liquidity Sweep]] — a BOS frequently follows or accompanies a liquidity sweep; sweep + BOS = institutional
- [[Swing Low]] — a bearish BOS requires a close below a swing low; mark swing lows to anticipate BOSes
- [[Kill Zones]] — BOSes that occur inside kill zones have much more follow-through than midday breaks
- [[Power of Three]] — the distribution phase of PO3 typically begins with a BOS on the lower timeframe
- [[IFVG]] — the displacement that creates a BOS often also closes through an existing FVG, forming an IFVG
- [[Breaker Block]] — when a BOS breaks through an existing OB, it converts that OB into a breaker block
