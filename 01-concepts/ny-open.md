# NY Open

## Definition
The NY Open kill zone runs **13:30–16:00 GMT** (08:30–11:00 EST). This is the window for the **biggest directional moves** of the day, particularly around scheduled news events (NFP, FOMC, CPI). The NY open amplifies whatever direction London set, or reverses London if the HTF setup demands it.

The typical NY open pattern:
1. London set a directional move (or a range)
2. At 13:30 GMT, a second liquidity sweep or continuation move occurs
3. Price runs decisively toward the [[Draw on Liquidity]] for the day

*Example:* London bullishly reversed off the Asian session low. Price has been grinding higher. At 13:32 GMT (NY open), a small pullback sweeps a minor low, creates a 1M [[Fair Value Gap]], then surges higher toward the daily target. Enter long in the FVG.

## Rules
- The 13:30–14:30 GMT window (first hour of NY) is the most explosive
- Watch for a brief sweep or pullback at open — this creates the entry, not the initial surge
- NY open setups around economic news are the highest probability AND highest risk — size down
- If London and NY are both bullish, the combination signal is extremely strong
- Avoid trading 12:00–13:15 GMT (pre-NY low-liquidity window) — choppy, unreliable price action
- After 16:00 GMT (11:00 EST), volume drops sharply — avoid new entries
- A [[Power of Three]] "distribution" phase often plays out from NY open to close
