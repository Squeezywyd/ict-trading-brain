# Best Setups

## Definition
These are the **highest probability trade setups** in this ICT system, ranked by confluence count and historical consistency. A "best" setup is not about the individual pattern — it is about the combination of aligned factors.

**The A+ Setup Formula:**
HTF bias ✓ + Kill zone timing ✓ + Liquidity sweep ✓ + Displacement ✓ + Entry model (FVG/IFVG/OB) ✓ + SMT (optional +1)

---

### Setup 1: PO3 London Sweep + IFVG (Highest Probability)
1. HTF (4H/1D) bias is clear
2. Asian session builds a tight range
3. London open sweeps one side of the Asian range
4. Strong displacement creates an IFVG
5. [[SMT Divergence]] confirms direction on correlated pair
6. Enter on IFVG retest with rejection

### Setup 2: HTF OB Retest at Kill Zone
1. HTF marks a clean, untouched [[Order Block (Bullish)|OB]] (all 4 criteria met)
2. Price retraces to the OB during London or NY kill zone
3. Lower TF shows a rejection (pin bar or engulfing) inside the OB
4. Enter with SL below OB low, TP at next liquidity pool

### Setup 3: Equal Highs/Lows Sweep + FVG
1. Clear [[Equal Highs]] or [[Equal Lows]] on the chart
2. Price sweeps the level during a kill zone
3. Displacement creates a clean [[Fair Value Gap]] after the sweep
4. Enter on FVG retest with rejection

## Rules
- If fewer than 3 of the 5 criteria are met, it is NOT an A+ setup — skip it
- The best setups feel like "waiting" — they are obvious in hindsight, boring in real time
- News events (NFP, FOMC) inside a kill zone with an aligned setup = extreme probability
- Never force a "best setup" by ignoring missing criteria — that turns it into a [[Worst Setups|worst setup]]
