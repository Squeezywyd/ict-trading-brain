# Worst Setups

## Definition
Worst setups are **trade entries with missing confluences, wrong timing, or structural misalignment** that retail traders frequently take because they *look* like valid setups. Understanding why these setups fail is as important as knowing the best setups.

---

### Worst Setup 1: Counter-Trend Without CHOCH
Trading short in a bullish trend (or long in a bearish trend) without a confirmed [[Change of Character]]. The pattern may look like a short — bearish OB, FVG, equal highs — but if HTF is bullish, you're fighting institutions.

### Worst Setup 2: Midday FVG Entry (Outside Kill Zone)
Entering a [[Fair Value Gap]] retest at 2:00 PM EST when there's no kill zone active. FVGs outside kill zones fill unpredictably and lack the volume needed for reliable rejection.

### Worst Setup 3: Mitigated OB Second Touch
Entering an [[Order Block (Bullish)|OB]] that was already mitigated on the first retest. The institutional orders are gone. The zone is a coincidence pattern, not a live order cluster.

### Worst Setup 4: Stacked FVG Mess
Entering when multiple [[Fair Value Gap|FVGs]] overlap without clear structure. ICT rule: overlapping FVGs = uncertainty. Pick none of them or zoom out to HTF.

### Worst Setup 5: Chasing After Displacement
Entering a breakout by buying after a [[Displacement Candle]] closes — not waiting for the FVG retest. You're entering at the top of the displacement, with poor risk:reward and your stop in a swept zone.

## Rules
- If any of these patterns describe your trade — stop and reassess
- The worst setups feel exciting and urgent — that urgency is a warning sign
- "FOMO" entries (Fear of Missing Out) are almost always worst-setup trades
- One bad setup can wipe multiple wins — reject low-quality setups without emotion
